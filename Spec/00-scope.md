
# BrainOps Language (BOL)
## Scope and Applicability (Normative)

This document defines the scope, boundaries, and applicability of BrainOps Language (BOL).

Keywords **MUST**, **MUST NOT**, **SHOULD**, **MAY** are normative.

---

## 1. Purpose

BOL is a formal language for:
- constraining thinking
- fixing cognitive decisions
- managing cognitive risk
- ensuring auditability of decisions

BOL is designed for systems where the **cost of error is non-trivial**.

---

## 2. In-Scope

BOL applies to:
- human decision-making processes
- AI-assisted decision systems
- multi-agent systems
- safety-critical workflows
- governance and review processes

---

## 3. Out-of-Scope

BOL does NOT define:
- user interface design
- natural language style
- creativity or ideation
- optimization strategies
- learning or model training

---

## 4. Non-Goals

BOL is NOT intended to:
- replace human judgment
- automate responsibility
- guarantee correctness
- provide ethical conclusions

---

## 5. Conditions of Use

BOL MUST NOT be used when:
- decisions cannot be verified
- error has no measurable cost
- outcomes are not recorded
- auditability is impossible

---

## 6. Authority

This repository is the **canonical source of truth** for BOL.
Any fork or derivative MUST clearly state deviations.
